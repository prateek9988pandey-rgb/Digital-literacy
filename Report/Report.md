## Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic

For this task, I created an infographic using Canva. The infographic explains what digital literacy is and why it is important for students. It includes key topics such as safe internet practices, useful digital tools, and building a professional online presence.  

Digital literacy means the ability to use technology safely, effectively, and responsibly. In my design, I included safety tips like using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and protecting personal information. I also added tools such as Google Docs, Zoom, and educational platforms that help students in learning.  

The most interesting part was designing the layout and choosing colors and icons. The challenging part was fitting all the information into one page without making it crowded. Overall, this task improved my creativity and understanding of visual communication.

## Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness

For this task, I studied cybercrime, especially phishing attacks. Phishing is a type of fraud where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information like passwords or bank details.  

These attacks usually happen through fake emails or messages. Students are often targeted because they may not be aware of such threats. The consequences can include financial loss and data theft.  

To prevent cybercrime, users should avoid clicking unknown links, use strong passwords, and verify sources before sharing information. Awareness is the best way to stay safe online.  

This task helped me understand real-world digital risks and how to protect myself and others.
